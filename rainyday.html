Project 1 - Rainy day

No financial gibberish, light on formalities, heavy on real-life utility and connection. The Rainy day mobile app functions as a financial advisor, tracking the user’s money movements with a particular focus on saving goals.

1. Overview
“Our brains have not developed for the purpose of making clever financial decisions.” (Mira Fauth-Bühler, Professor of Neuroeconomics)

Rainy day tries to build a plain-worded and trustworthy financial foundation for people. A tool that empathizes with real-life needs in a world where information overload and stimuli are abundant.

To help users save money while establishing Goals, the interface will use a vision board theory (Tara Stuart, Neuroscientist) to imprint important things to the user's subsconscious. The user will have each Goal identified by a chosen image that will work as a ‘value-trigger’ and, ultimately, fulfill the objective.

2. The process

A 5 week endeavor from research to the final UI, acting as the sole designer.


3. User Stories and Flow

  • User Story 1: As a money-saver, I need to be able to input information on the money I am receiving and spending (and on what), so that I can see an overview of my finances.

  • User Story 2: As a user, I want to see a dashboard of my finances clearly and visually, so that I can see how much I am spending on what at a glance.

  • User Story 3: As a user, I need to be able to tell the tool what my savings goal is and how long I have to reach it, so that I can save accordingly.


4. Lo-Fi and Mid-Fi Wireframes

Lo-Fi: The Goals’ key flow was first interpreted as a set of low fidelity sketches.

Mid-Fi: These were then translated to mid-fi wireframes and a working prototype that would be submitted to user testing.

5. Brand Guidelines

Rainy Day aims to be a financial shelter, a close supporting hand wherever and whenever finance is concerned. No formal mumbo-jumbo, no cryptic stats or crypto dross. We just help you save money for those special expenses that come once or thrice in life, come rain or shine.

This attitude reflected a simple approach to the brand’s logo. A bold expression, slight touches of personality but nevertheless simple. The symbol is an extension of the protection of the user’s goals. An umbrella to shelter, a rainbow for financial hope.

In a future iteration, an in-app insurance plan to cover goals could be pertinent.

6. User Feedback

The Mid-Fi Prototype was tested with 5 participants, providing a key scenario and context for the mobile app. The identified issues were addressed - ranging from readability concerns to navigation problems -, proceeding to the Hi-Fi Wireframes and final mockups.


Provided Scenario: You’ve downloaded and logged in to the Rainy day app to start improving your financial management. Now, you have arrived at the home screen’s dashboard and want to add a new saving goal, establishing a budget and a date for that said goal.

- Revised general copy and mathematical typos for better understanding (Goals %);

- Text size was revised and tested again;

- Revised the amount component when adding a goal to 6 digits. Target-wise, I did not consider the 1% of population that could manage one-million budgets but, even so, designed for that edge-case;

- Revised whole iconography for navigation and logo system to be more consistent and simple;

- Removed a mobile breadcrumb component, while maintaining a progress bar throughout the goal flow;

- Overlay was added to the Hi-Fi Prototype on the goal’s detail screen which allows the user to edit or remove the goal. This function is not immediately accessible on the “My Goals” overview screen since it could induce easily accessible errors after saving so much money;

7. Final UI

Onboarding Flow: A splash animation was created to welcome the user's onboarding, followed by a set of 3 screens that would contextualize the app's main features.


Goal Flow: From the Goals screen, the user can enter each Goal's detail, add / withdraw or move money in between goals. The user can also edit his Goal’s information which can only be accessed when entering this detail state.


Desktop: A desktop webpage was created to market the mobile app, showcasing its branding, tone of voice and main functionality. Due note to the adaptation of the main dashboard to desktop resolution.


8. Reflections

During my 5 weeks Rainy day journey, a lot has happened. I felt the need to thoroughly examine the competitor space on each advancing task, hands-on through a myriad of apps (N26, Revolut, Qapital, Chime), while iterating on the branding aspects many times. Ultimately I felt the need to consult my mentor to consolidate my color system and card design anatomy which had to function on odd and even numbers on a responsive grid.

While working on the desktop page, the project became more solid. A brand voice helped me feel that the project breathed some manner of life, acknowledging my previous experience. With more time and necessary user feedback, I’d like to design the Stats and Wallet Flow on a 2nd iteration. I also believe that many focal technicalities should be addressed (from payment information linkage to the important settings that a financial app must have as crystal-clear).
